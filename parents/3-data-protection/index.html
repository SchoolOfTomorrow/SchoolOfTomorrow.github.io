Data protection

The sampled data will be handled in accordance with the Swiss legal basis for data protection. The data will not be passed on to commercial companies for advertising purposes. The researchers involved in the project will not make comparisons between individual study participants. Personal achievements will not be communicated. The results will be communicated in internationally recognized scientific journals and at conferences. Data will always be reported in anonymised form. Based on published data, it will not be possible to infer the identity of a person participating in the study. Data from laboratory tests will not be passed on to teachers in order to prevent any influence on the grading. After completion of the project, the data will be made available to the scientific community in anonymised form. This is to ensure that the data can be analyzed by other scientists and that there is absolute transparency with regard to the conclusions we draw, so that the social benefit of the study carried out is as great as possible.
